## 16. QUALITY ASSURANCE

### 16.1 Service Quality Standards

Saypeace commits to maintaining the highest standards of service quality in all aspects of this Agreement.

**16.1.1 Performance Standards**

- **System Uptime:** 99.5% minimum availability
- **Response Times:** Adherence to SLA response timeframes
- **Resolution Times:** Meet or exceed resolution targets
- **Content Quality:** Error-free, properly formatted content
- **Security:** Maintain robust security posture
- **Communication:** Clear, timely, professional communication

**16.1.2 Quality Metrics**

Saypeace shall track and report on:
- Uptime percentage
- Average response time by priority
- Average resolution time by priority
- SLA compliance rate
- Content turnaround time
- Customer satisfaction scores
- Incident frequency and severity

### 16.2 Quality Assurance Processes

**16.2.1 Testing Procedures**

All system changes undergo:

**Development Testing:**
- Unit testing of individual components
- Integration testing of combined features
- Functional testing of complete features

**Staging Environment Testing:**
- Deploy to staging environment first
- Comprehensive testing in production-like environment
- Cross-browser testing
- Mobile responsiveness testing
- Performance testing

**User Acceptance Testing (UAT):**
- Client review and testing when applicable
- Feedback incorporation
- Final approval before production deployment

**Production Deployment:**
- Scheduled during low-traffic periods
- Rollback plan prepared
- Post-deployment verification
- Monitoring for issues

**16.2.2 Content Quality Assurance**

Before publishing content, Saypeace verifies:
- Proper formatting and styling
- Image optimization and display
- Link functionality
- Mobile responsiveness
- SEO elements (meta tags, descriptions)
- Grammar and spelling (basic check)
- Brand consistency

**16.2.3 Security Quality Assurance**

Regular security assessments:
- Monthly vulnerability scans
- Quarterly penetration testing
- Annual comprehensive security audit
- Continuous security monitoring
- Security patch testing before deployment

### 16.3 Continuous Improvement

**16.3.1 Performance Review**

Quarterly review of:
- Service delivery performance

- Process optimization opportunities

**16.3.2 Feedback Mechanism**

## 17. TERMINATION CLAUSES

### 17.1 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason by providing **NINETY (90) DAYS** written notice to the other Party.

**17.1.1 Termination by Client**

ABU Business School may terminate by:
- Providing 90 days written notice
- Paying all outstanding fees through termination date
- Paying early termination fee of **THREE (3) MONTHS** service fees
- Cooperating in transition and handover

**Early termination fee waived if:**
- Terminating after Initial Term (5 years)
- Material breach by Saypeace (not cured within 30 days)
- Force majeure lasting over 90 days
- Mutual agreement to waive

**17.1.2 Termination by Saypeace**

Saypeace may terminate by:
- Providing 90 days written notice
- Continuing services through notice period
- Assisting in transition to new provider
- No termination fee charged to Client

### 17.2 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate immediately for cause in the following situations:

**17.2.1 Material Breach**

If either Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **THIRTY (30) DAYS** of written notice.

**Examples of Material Breach by Saypeace:**
- Repeated failure to meet SLA commitments
- Data breach due to gross negligence
- Unauthorized use of Client data
- Abandonment of service
- Insolvency or bankruptcy

**Examples of Material Breach by Client:**
- Non-payment for more than 30 days after due date
- Unauthorized use or redistribution of system
- Breach of confidentiality obligations
- Interference with Saypeace's service delivery
- Insolvency or bankruptcy

**17.2.2 Immediate Termination Events**

Either Party may terminate immediately without cure period if:
- Other Party becomes insolvent or bankrupt
- Other Party commits fraud or criminal activity
- Other Party breaches confidentiality causing material harm
- Government action prohibits performance
- Force majeure lasting more than 90 consecutive days

### 17.3 Effects of Termination

**17.3.1 Immediate Effects**

Upon termination:
- All outstanding invoices become immediately due
- Service continues through paid period or notice period (whichever is longer)
- No new services or commitments initiated
- Transition planning commences

**17.3.2 Transition Assistance (60-Day Period)**

Saypeace shall provide:

**Documentation:**
- Complete system documentation
- Configuration details
- User manuals and SOPs
- Network architecture diagrams
- Security protocols

**Data Export:**
- Complete database export (SQL dump)
- All uploaded files and media
- System configurations
- User accounts and permissions
- Analytics data

**Knowledge Transfer:**
- Up to 10 hours of knowledge transfer sessions
- Q&A support during transition
- Technical guidance for new provider
- Handover meeting with new provider (if requested)

Additional transition assistance beyond 10 hours: ₦8,000/hour

**17.3.3 Client Obligations Upon Termination**

ABU Business School shall:
- Pay all outstanding fees and charges
- Pay early termination fee if applicable
- Cooperate in transition process
- Designate contact person for handover
- Not disparage Saypeace publicly

**17.3.4 Saypeace Obligations Upon Termination**

Saypeace shall:
- Continue services through paid period
- Provide transition assistance
- Deliver all Client data and documentation
- Not retain copies of Client data beyond 90 days
- Securely destroy all Client data after retention period
- Not disparage Client publicly

**17.3.5 Data Deletion**

Unless otherwise agreed:
- Client data retained for **90 DAYS** after termination
- Client may request data export during retention period
- After 90 days, all Client data securely deleted
- Certificate of deletion provided upon request

**17.3.6 System Deactivation**

After termination and data retention period:
- Client access to systems terminated
- Hosting services discontinued
- Domain transferred to Client or released
- SSL certificates transferred or cancelled

### 17.4 Survival of Terms

The following provisions survive termination:
- Payment obligations for services rendered
- Confidentiality obligations (5 years)
- Intellectual property rights
- Limitation of liability
- Dispute resolution procedures
- Data protection obligations

### 17.5 No Waiver of Rights

Delay or failure to enforce any provision does not constitute waiver of that right or any other right under this Agreement.

## 18. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 18.1 Dispute Resolution Process

The Parties agree to resolve disputes through the following graduated process:

**18.1.1 Step 1: Direct Negotiation (14 Days)**

**Process:**
- Party raising dispute provides written notice describing the issue
- Designated representatives meet within 7 days
- Good faith negotiations to resolve dispute
- Document resolution or impasse

**Representatives:**
- For Client: Designated administrative contact
- For Saypeace: Project Manager or Senior Management

**Timeline:** 14 days from dispute notice

**18.1.2 Step 2: Senior Management Negotiation (14 Days)**

If Step 1 fails:

**Process:**
- Escalate to senior management of both Parties
- Meeting between:
  - Client: Dean or designated senior official
  - Saypeace: CEO (Engr. Salisu Zubairu Gaya)
- Good faith negotiations
- Document resolution or impasse

**Timeline:** 14 days from Step 1 conclusion

**18.1.3 Step 3: Mediation (30 Days)**

If Step 2 fails:

**Process:**
- Engage neutral third-party mediator
- Both Parties participate in mediation sessions
- Mediator facilitates resolution discussions
- Non-binding recommendations

**Mediator Selection:**
- Mutually agreed mediator, or
- Mediator appointed by [Kaduna State Arbitration Center] or similar body

**Costs:**
- Mediator fees shared equally
- Each Party bears own legal costs

**Timeline:** 30 days from Step 2 conclusion

**18.1.4 Step 4: Arbitration (Final Resolution)**

If mediation fails:

**Arbitration Terms:**
- Final and binding arbitration
- Governed by Nigerian Arbitration and Conciliation Act
- Venue: Zaria or Kaduna, Nigeria
- Language: English
- Single arbitrator mutually agreed, or appointed by recognized arbitration body
- Arbitrator's decision is final and binding
- Each Party bears own legal costs unless arbitrator rules otherwise
- Arbitrator fees shared equally unless arbitrator rules otherwise

**Arbitration Process:**
- Submit to arbitration within 30 days of mediation conclusion
- Arbitrator appointed within 14 days
- Discovery period as determined by arbitrator
- Hearing scheduled at arbitrator's discretion
- Decision rendered within 60 days of hearing completion

### 18.2 Expedited Resolution for Urgent Matters

For disputes requiring immediate resolution (e.g., security issues, system outages):
- Skip to senior management negotiation (Step 2)
- If unresolved within 48 hours, proceed to expedited arbitration
- Arbitrator to hear matter within 7 days
- Decision within 14 days

### 18.3 Continued Performance During Disputes

**18.3.1 Service Continuity**

Unless the dispute concerns immediate safety or security:
- Services continue during dispute resolution
- Both Parties fulfill contractual obligations
- Payment obligations continue
- Good faith cooperation maintained

**18.3.2 Exceptions**

Services may be suspended only if:
- Payment is more than 30 days overdue
- Security threat from other Party
- Court or arbitrator orders suspension
- Mutual written agreement

### 18.4 Governing Law and Jurisdiction

**18.4.1 Governing Law**

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the **Federal Republic of Nigeria**.

**18.4.2 Jurisdiction**

Subject to the dispute resolution procedures above, the courts of **Kaduna State, Nigeria** shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any legal proceedings.

**18.4.3 Language**

All dispute resolution proceedings shall be conducted in English.

### 18.5 Injunctive Relief

Notwithstanding the dispute resolution process, either Party may seek injunctive relief or other equitable remedies in court for:
- Breach of confidentiality
- Intellectual property infringement
- Imminent irreparable harm
- Urgent security matters

Such action does not waive the arbitration requirement for monetary damages or other relief.

### 18.6 Limitation Period

Any claim arising from this Agreement must be brought within **TWO (2) YEARS** of the date the Party became aware (or should reasonably have become aware) of the facts giving rise to the claim.

## 19. FORCE MAJEURE

### 19.1 Definition

"**Force Majeure Event**" means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party that prevents or delays that Party from performing its obligations under this Agreement, including but not limited to:

**Natural Disasters:**
- Earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, tornadoes
- Fires, lightning strikes
- Severe weather conditions

**Human-Caused Events:**
- War, terrorism, civil unrest, riots
- Strikes, labor disputes (not involving Party's own employees)
- Government actions, regulations, or orders
- Epidemics, pandemics, public health emergencies

**Technical Events:**
- Failure of third-party infrastructure (internet backbone, power grid)
- Telecommunications failures
- Cyber attacks or DDoS attacks (if beyond reasonable security measures)

**Other Events:**
- Acts of God
- Any other event beyond reasonable control

### 19.2 Exclusions

Force Majeure does **NOT** include:
- Financial difficulties or inability to pay
- Labor disputes with Party's own employees
- Failure of suppliers or subcontractors (unless due to Force Majeure)
- Technical failures due to inadequate systems or maintenance
- Events that could have been prevented by reasonable precautions
- Performance delays caused by Party's negligence

### 19.3 Notification Requirements

**19.3.1 Initial Notification**

Party affected by Force Majeure must:
- Notify the other Party **within 48 hours** of becoming aware
- Provide written notice describing:
  - Nature of the Force Majeure Event
  - Expected duration of impact
  - Affected obligations
  - Steps being taken to mitigate impact

**19.3.2 Ongoing Updates**

- Provide weekly updates on status
- Notify immediately when Force Majeure ends
- Provide notice if duration extends beyond initial estimate

### 19.4 Effects of Force Majeure

**19.4.1 Suspension of Obligations**

During Force Majeure:
- Affected Party's obligations suspended to the extent prevented by the event
- Non-affected Party's obligations also suspended proportionally
- Time for performance extended by duration of Force Majeure
- No liability for failure to perform suspended obligations

**19.4.2 Mitigation Obligations**

Party affected by Force Majeure must:
- Use reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact
- Implement backup and contingency plans
- Resume performance as soon as reasonably possible
- Minimize duration and impact

**19.4.3 Payment During Force Majeure**

- If services substantially continue: Full payment due
- If services partially unavailable: Pro-rata payment based on availability
- If services completely unavailable: Payment suspended for duration
- Service credits not applicable for Force Majeure-caused downtime

### 19.5 Termination Due to Force Majeure

**19.5.1 Extended Force Majeure**

If Force Majeure continues for more than **NINETY (90) CONSECUTIVE DAYS**:
- Either Party may terminate Agreement with 14 days written notice
- No early termination fees apply
- Payment for services rendered up to termination date
- Transition assistance provided on best-efforts basis

**19.5.2 Effects of Termination**

- Provisions of Section 17.3 (Effects of Termination) apply
- Extended timelines if Force Majeure continues
- Good faith cooperation despite circumstances
- No liability for Force Majeure-caused delays

### 19.6 COVID-19 and Pandemic Considerations

In the event of pandemic-related restrictions:
- Remote service delivery continues where possible
- In-person meetings converted to virtual
- On-site support provided remotely
- Training sessions conducted online
- Documentation replaces certain in-person requirements
